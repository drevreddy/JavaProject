Passengers with Special Needs


A medical information form (MEDIF) is required to be completed by passengers with special needs so that their travel by Bharat Airlines can be made comfortable, eg.

  • Those who need a stretcher or incubator on board the aircraft;
  • Those whose medical condition requires oxygen supply on board the aircraft;
  • Those who require extra space so that they can have their legs raised in flight;
  • Those who cannot take care of themselves on the ground or during the flight;
  • Those who require the carriage and use of medical equipment on board the aircraft; and
  • Passengers who are considered unfit to travel unescorted, e.g. old age compounded with osteoarthritis and language problems.

Contraindications for Air Travel

  • Anaemia of severe degree (Haemoglobin less than 7 gm%).
  • Severe case of otitis media (infection of the ears) and sinusitis.
  • Acute contagious or communicable disease e.g. persons who are suspected or suffering from cholera, dysentery, typhoid fever, paratyphoid fever, chickenpox, active tuberculosis or any other epidemic infections or any contagious diseases are not accepted for air travel.
  • Skin diseases that are contagious or repulsive in appearance.
  • Uncomplicated Myocardial Infarction within one week of onset; uncomplicated Congestive Cardiac Failure within one week of onset or unless stabilised.
  • Hypertensive disease with severe complications.
  • Peptic ulceration with recent haemorrhage within 3 weeks.
  • Post-operative cases

Within 10 days of simple abdominal operations
Within 21 days of chest surgery.

  • Fractures of the mandible with fixed wiring of the jaw.
  • Mental illness without escort and sedation. Those who are mentally ill and whose medical condition is unstable; alcoholics or intoxicated to other drugs. Psychiatric conditions. Severely anxious, depressed or psychotic patients should be advised against air travel. The stress of travel may exacerbate their condition and cause difficulty or danger to themselves, or to other passengers. Less severely ill patients may be able to travel, provided they are escorted and have supplies of medication available should their condition deteriorate during travel. A psychiatric assessment prior to travel may be necessary.
  • Infants within seven days of birth.
  • Pregnant women whose confinement may be expected in less than 28 days i.e. after 32 weeks of pregnancy. Expectant mothers between 32 to 36 weeks of pregnancy are not required to have an escort by a physician if the attending physician certifies fitness for air travel.
  • Cerebral Vascular Accidents (Stroke) within one week.

Although these are suggested limiting factors, each individual case will be considered on its merits and dependent on whether or not the passenger is accompanied by a professional escort.
