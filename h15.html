Bharat Airlines, India’s premier low cost airline lays a lot of emphasis on proper baggage handling and it is our constant endeavor to make sure that we have the least number of short shipped bags. Like most other airlines, our baggage handling is outsourced to outside agencies and at times bags do get misplaced. For your convenience we have listed below a few pointers on how to avoid misplacement of baggage along with our baggage policies.

Baggage Handling

  1. Is there any specific advice to passengers to avoid lost baggage incidents on Bharat Airlines?
    It is advised to all passengers flying by any airline to label their luggage with their complete name, address and contact details for easy identification. It would be even better if the label is made in large and readable font.

    All passengers should ensure that their bags are locked and closed properly.


 

  1. How much baggage can I check-in on Bharat Airlines flights?
    Each passenger (excluding infants) is allowed a maximum of 15 kgs checked in baggage.

  2. Is there a special baggage allowance for international passengers?
    Bharat Airlines does not have a special baggage allowance for international passengers. The baggage allowance is the same for all passengers as mentioned above.

 

  1. Are prams and infant seats allowed to be carried on Bharat Airlines flights?
    Baby seats and prams are not allowed on board Bharat Airlines flights.






 

  1. With upgraded security on aircraft what items are not permitted to be carried onboard the aircraft?
    Recently, there has been an increase in the level of security onboard all aircraft. Passengers are not allowed to bring any sharp objects onto the aircraft. This includes nail clippers, scissors, razors, blades, bulbs, batteries or any other object that may be considered dangerous. The airline will not be held responsible for any such item, which is taken over by airport security agencies.



 

 

  1. What should I do if my checked in baggage does not reach the destination?
    In the event of your baggage not reaching the destination on time, immediately report to the airport counter at the destination. Please fill the ‘property irregularity report’ (PIR) completely and submit at the counter. Please note that the PIR has to be submitted immediately upon arrival and no baggage complaint will be registered after the passenger has left the airport. Please ensure that the PIR has a detailed description of the lost baggage for easy identification.

 

 

7. What procedure is followed by Bharat Airlines for identifying, recovering and returning lost baggage?
Bharat Airlines usually ensures that the baggage is traced in 2 days and handed over to the passenger. In the event of loss or damage to the baggage, Bharat Airlines will compensate the passenger at Rs 450/- per kilo, provided, loss of baggage has been reported to the airline, prior to leaving the airport on the day of the loss and if not traced within 7 days. Bharat Airlines is not liable for any damage or loss of hand baggage.
